# IPC: Public Order and State-Related Offences

Chapter VIII (Sections 141 to 160) covers offences against public tranquility.
Section 141 defines unlawful assembly as an assembly of five or more persons with one of the listed common objects.
Section 146 defines rioting as use of force or violence by an unlawful assembly or any member.

Section 149 creates constructive liability for offences committed by members of an unlawful assembly in prosecution of common object.

Sections 503 and 506 cover criminal intimidation and punishment.
Sections 499 and 500 cover defamation and punishment.

Sections 121 onwards concern offences against the State, including waging war and related acts.
These provisions involve high thresholds and serious punishments.
